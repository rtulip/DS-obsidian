## Birth of the Heartless King

Before the name **Threxian Voss** was ever spoken in fear, there was **Tarian Voss**: a learned sovereign, philosopher-prince, and bitter enemy of death’s dominion over the soul.

Tarian’s ruin began with the discovery of an artifact known as **the Mortal Coil**, a relic of impossible power said to make the immortal mortal. Where others saw mercy in such a thing, Tarian saw only a curse. To him, the Coil was not a gift of release, but a weapon against eternity itself. If even the deathless could be dragged back into the reach of age, pain, and decay, then no ascension was secure. No godhood, sainthood, or immortality could truly be trusted while the Mortal Coil endured.

So Tarian sought to destroy it.

Years of forbidden study revealed a terrible truth: the Mortal Coil could not be shattered by mortal hands, arcane fire, royal decree, or infernal bargain. It could only be destroyed by **Godly Power**. Denied such power himself, Tarian turned to manipulation. He founded a secret order called **the Hollow Crown**, a cult devoted publicly to the rejection of false mercy, the sanctity of eternal rule, and the coming of a deathless king. In truth, the cult existed for one purpose: to draw the wrath of the living saint **Tovian, the Bellringer**.

Through staged atrocities, desecrated shrines, and blasphemous rites, the Hollow Crown became a wound in the world too loud for Tovian to ignore. When the saint came at last, bearing holy judgment and the tolling power of the divine bell, Tarian sprang his design. The Mortal Coil was placed at the heart of the confrontation, and Tovian, believing he was striking down the cult’s greatest profane relic, unleashed the Godly Power required to destroy it.

The Mortal Coil was broken.

With the one artifact capable of threatening immortality erased from existence, Tarian abandoned all pretense of noble purpose. He descended fully into necromancy, binding his soul beyond death and carving the weakness of his mortal life from himself. His final rite of lichdom was said to include the removal of his own heart, not as a sacrifice, but as a declaration.

Tarian Voss died that night.

In his place rose **Threxian Voss, the Heartless King**: lich, sovereign of the Hollow Crown, and master of an eternity no mortal mercy could ever again unmake.
