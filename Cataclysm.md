At the height of the Hollow Crown’s early terror, **Tovian, the Bellringer** came to the city of **Capital** in pursuit of the cult’s hidden master, [[Threxian Voss, the Heartless King|Tarian Voss]]. Beneath the city, in a sanctum prepared with deliberate care, Tovian and his [[Order of the Closed Ear]] found a necromantic machine of great power fueled by a great unknown power. To him, it appeared to be the heart of the cult’s blasphemy: a profane relic around which the Hollow Crown had gathered its rites, sacrifices, and prayers.

In truth, it was a trap.

When the living saint called down divine force upon the heart of the machinery, he believed he was ending the Hollow Crown’s greatest evil. The sound that followed was heard across the whole of Capital: not as a ringing in the ears, but as a tolling in the bones.

In that instant, time convulsed.

Every living thing within the city aged as though fifty years had passed in a single breath. Children became bent and white-haired. The young found their strength stolen. The middle-aged collapsed into frailty. The elderly withered where they stood, many crumbling to dust before their families could reach them. Trees split and hollowed. Horses fell in the streets. Tovian survived the blast, though witnesses claimed he emerged from the ruined sanctum aged, bloodied, and silent. Some accounts say he tried to heal the survivors until his hands shook too badly to hold them. Others claim he simply walked through the streets, listening to the grief he had caused and offering no defense.

His follower first called the disaster a mystery. Then they called it a tragedy. Finally, they named it a sin. The Bellringer was no longer trusted to have justice ring true throughout the lands.
